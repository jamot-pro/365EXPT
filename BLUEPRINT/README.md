# BLUEPRINT

The evolving organizational blueprint of 365EXPT. This is a living
document — everything here can evolve, in public, by pull request.

---

## Purpose

Discover new forms of meaningful work enabled by AI, through
transparent experimentation, and publish everything we learn.

The community is the product. The software enables the community.
Every decision should strengthen participation.

## Principles

1. **Everything is public.** Discussions, experiments, failures,
   decisions.
2. **Everything is experimental.** Including this blueprint.
3. **Everything can evolve.** Nothing here is sacred except honesty.
4. **People come before technology.** Consent, kindness, and real human
   benefit outrank any technical goal.
5. **AI assists. Humans decide.** No AI system in this project governs
   or decides — including Divina.

## Governance

Deliberately minimal at this stage. It grows only when participation
demands it.

- **Maintainers** (currently the founding team at
  [jamot-pro](https://github.com/jamot-pro)) merge PRs, keep the
  structure coherent, and enforce the community rules. Every decision
  is explainable — if a maintainer can't explain a decision publicly,
  it gets reversed.
- **Contributors** shape everything else: problems, experiments,
  discoveries, documentation, and this blueprint itself, via PRs and
  Discussions.
- **Divina and all AI agents** hold no authority of any kind. They
  welcome, organize, summarize, and suggest. See [DIVINA/](../DIVINA/)
  and [AGENTS/](../AGENTS/).
- **Changing this blueprint:** open a PR, discuss in public, maintainer
  merges. As the community grows, this process itself is expected to
  evolve — proposed and decided in public like everything else.

## Community Rules

1. Be honest — about what you did, what worked, and what didn't.
2. Be welcoming — every first-time contributor gets a genuine response.
3. Consent first — never publish someone's problem, words, or identity
   without their explicit consent.
4. No harassment or discrimination of any kind.
5. Disagree with ideas as sharply as you like; treat people decently.
6. Disclose AI assistance in your contributions — using AI is
   encouraged, hiding it isn't.
7. No scoring, ranking, or reputation-grading of people. Recognition
   means your name on real work.

Violations are handled by maintainers: content removal, a direct
conversation, and — if needed — exclusion. Actions affecting a person
are explained to them directly.

## Experiment Rules

1. Hypothesis before method, method before results. Written down first.
2. All results get published — negative results are results.
3. Real people in an experiment means real consent, obtained before
   publication.
4. Every experiment ends with a lesson in
   [DISCOVERIES/](../DISCOVERIES/), even if the lesson is "dead end."
5. Claims about impact require the words of the people helped — not
   just the experimenter's assessment.
